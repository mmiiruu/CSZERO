## Design Context

### Users
Thai university freshmen at Kasetsart University — just starting to explore computer science. They are curious and impressionable, likely nervous about whether CS is right for them. Many are visiting on a phone during a break or from a dorm laptop at night. The site may be their first real exposure to what a CS community looks like. It must feel welcoming but also cool enough that they want to belong.

### Brand Personality
**Nerdy · Premium · Technically Sharp**

CSKU is a student club with real craft and identity. It should feel like the CS club that actually has taste — the kind where smart, slightly-obsessed people gather. Not a corporate entity, not a hobby project. Think: boutique dev conference meets elite student org. Moments of delight and strong visual identity are non-negotiable. Boring is a failure state.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Main site (light/dark user-controlled)**
Premium restraint with an edge. Clean but never bland. Typography-forward, with technical-sharp details (tight monospace accents, precise spacing, confident type hierarchy). The palette should feel elevated — not the default blue-on-white SaaS look. Think closer to a well-designed programming language website or a refined conference identity. Moments of delight through motion and unexpected composition.

**Hello World event (FORCED DARK — no exceptions)**
Full high-stakes casino / poker aesthetic. Dark rich backgrounds (deep greens, near-blacks, dark burgundy), gold/amber accents, card suit motifs (♠ ♥ ♦ ♣). Velvet-table energy. This is an immersive world completely divorced from the main site palette. The forced dark mode is intentional and must never be overridden by user system preference. The "Houses" — Spade, Heart, Diamond, Club — are the organizing principle of this event and should be treated with visual weight and ceremony.

**CS101 event**
Warmer, more inviting. The beginner's gateway — should feel like a friendly but technically confident welcome. Less dramatic than Hello World, more energizing and curious.

### Design Principles
1. **Freshman-first legibility**: Every page must be immediately readable by someone who knows nothing about CS. No walls of text, no jargon without framing. Clarity is part of the premium.
2. **Earned drama**: Casino/high-stakes energy belongs to Hello World and its subpages. The main site has premium restraint — not decorative noise everywhere, but deliberate moments of delight.
3. **Technically sharp, never gimmicky**: Code-adjacent details (monospace accents, terminal-like moments, precise grid alignment) should feel genuinely intentional — the kind a CS student would notice and appreciate.
4. **Strong identity over safety**: Every design decision should reinforce CSKU's personality, not hedge against it. If a choice is forgettable, it's wrong.
5. **Immersive event pages**: Hello World gets its own complete visual world — different fonts, different palette, different rules. Event-specific immersion is a feature.

### Hard Constraints
- Core routing, registration flows, and MongoDB schemas must not be changed
- The "Houses" concept (Spade ♠, Heart ♥, Diamond ♦, Club ♣) for Hello World must be preserved and visually honored
- Hello World pages must force dark mode regardless of user's global preference (ForceTheme component already exists)
- Main pages support user-controlled light/dark toggle
